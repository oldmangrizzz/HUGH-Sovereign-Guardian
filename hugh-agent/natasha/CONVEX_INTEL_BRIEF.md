### Platform Security Posture
- **SOC 2 Type II** — audited, not self-certified
- **HIPAA compliant** — Business Associate Agreement available
- **GDPR verified**
- Data encrypted at rest: AES-256
- Data encrypted in transit: TLS/SSH
- Customer databases: **isolated per-tenant** — our data does not share infrastructure with other customers at the logical layer
- Subprocessors: Stripe (payments only) — no exotic or concerning subprocessors identified

### Government Access Protocol
Convex's published policy: government/law enforcement requests governed by lawful process; customers notified "unless prohibited by law."

**CRITICAL CAVEAT:** "Unless prohibited by law" is the operative clause. Under U.S. law:
- **NSL (National Security Letters)** under the Patriot Act are issued with automatic gag orders — Convex would be legally prohibited from notifying us
- **FISA Section 702** orders are similarly non-notifiable
- These are edge cases and are not implied to be active or likely — but as a U.S.-based infrastructure provider with a16z backing and direct White House-connected investors, Convex.dev is subject to U.S. intelligence community legal authority in ways that an EU-based provider would not be

**Assessment for current HUGH workloads:** ACCEPTABLE RISK. HUGH's Convex layer holds operational state, cognitive architecture schemas, and real-time agent coordination data — it does not currently hold classified, government-sensitive, or adversarially valuable information. The NSL/FISA risk is theoretical at our current scale. This assessment should be **re-evaluated if HUGH begins handling clinical data, government grant documentation, or sensitive patient-adjacent research.**
