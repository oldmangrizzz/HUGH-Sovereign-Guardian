## § ROBERT BARCLAY HANSON II — PATERNAL GRANDFATHER

### Confirmed Findings

**Born:** December 30, 1916 — **Maryland** (not West Virginia)
**Died:** December 19, 1958 — Fort Worth, Tarrant County, Texas. Age 41. Died 11 days before his 42nd birthday.
**Buried:** Greenwood Memorial Park and Mausoleum, Fort Worth, Tarrant County, Texas
**Source:** FindAGrave Memorial #89169445, created April 26, 2012 by contributor Cheryl Crump, with linked Ancestry records
**Confidence:** HIGH

**Father:** Harold Robert Hanson (1881–1918) — buried Maryland; died when Bob was approximately 18 months old. Cause of death presumed WWI-era, unconfirmed.
**Mother:** Hazel Dorothy McElhenie Mendenhall (1885–1964) — remarried after Harold's death; surname became Mendenhall.
**Half-sibling:** John Harold Mendenhall (1925–1926) — died in infancy.
**Source:** FindAGrave #89169445 family members panel
**Confidence:** HIGH

**Namesake note:** The name "Robert Barclay Hanson **II**" does not derive from his father Harold — Harold's name was different. The "II" traces to his **paternal grandfather** Robert Barclay Hanson (FindAGrave #98432097, buried Maryland), buried alongside Sarah Jane Hanson. Bob never knew his father; his patrilineal identity was rooted in his grandfather's line, not his father's.

**Marriage:** Married Frances Virginia Munro, **1938**. A Wisconsin marriage record is linked in Ancestry to this memorial, suggesting the ceremony may have occurred in Wisconsin — consistent with Virginia's family being in the Chicago area at that time.
**Source:** FindAGrave #89169445 spouse field; Ancestry-linked Wisconsin Marriage Records 1820–2015
**Confidence:** HIGH (marriage year); MEDIUM (Wisconsin venue — inferred from linked record type)

### Confirmed Conflicts With Prior Assumptions

- Bob was born in **Maryland**, not West Virginia. The WV connection appears to be ancestral, not his birthplace.
- Bob's "II" designation connects to his **grandfather**, not his father. His father Harold died before Bob could have known him.

### Gaps — Not Resolved

- Cause of death not retrieved (Texas Death Certificate behind Ancestry paywall)
- Exact Fort Worth address not confirmed
- Military service record not retrieved
- Date of Bob's arrival in Fort Worth not confirmed (likely followed Virginia's father c. 1948)
- Iowa state census link attached to his memorial unexplained — no documented Iowa connection for Bob or Virginia

### Open Leads

1. Ancestry 1950 Census (linked to #89169445) — will give Fort Worth address, household members, children's ages at time
2. Texas Death Certificate via Ancestry — cause of death, informant name, exact address at time of death

**Source:** Biola CE Database, citing Walker memorial sermon (1949)
**Confidence:** HIGH

**Second wife:** **Dorothy Hanson** — described as "an English professor from Baylor University." Married Harry between 1949 and 1955.
**Source:** Biola CE Database
**Confidence:** HIGH
**⚠ FLAG — See Cross-Target Intelligence section below.**

**Academic and institutional career:**

- Professorship at **Brite College of the Bible, Texas Christian University (TCU)**, Fort Worth — 1948–1955

**Source:** Biola CE Database; The Christian Century (June 1950); The Jarvisonian (1958); Sparks (1972)

**Source:** https://openlibrary.org/authors/OL2256282A/Harry_C._Munro
**Confidence:** HIGH

**Family biography published:** *Consider This Man: An Intimate Portrait of Harry C. Munro* — written by his daughter **Anna Laura (Munro) Gebhard**, published by Bethany Press, 1965, 40 pages. Amazon ASIN B0006P7HSC. Used copies available; not digitized. ## § CROSS-TARGET INTELLIGENCE

### The Dorothy Hanson Anomaly

The single architecturally significant finding in this sweep: **Harry C. Munro married Dorothy Hanson** (Baylor English professor) as his second wife after 1949. His son-in-law was **Robert Barclay Hanson II**. Both families carry the Hanson surname.

Whether Dorothy and Bob were related — cousins, siblings, or family friends — is **unconfirmed and requires direct investigation**. If related, the Munro-Hanson marriage of 1938 may have been partly a **convergence of two existing family networks** with prior ties, not a cold introduction. This is the most operationally interesting structural question in the entire family tree.

**Immediate action:** FindAGrave search for Dorothy Hanson Munro — her record will show maiden name vs. married name and any geographic indicator.

### The Fort Worth Nexus

All primary family members end in Fort Worth, Texas:
- Harry Munro → Fort Worth 1948 (TCU/Brite College)
- Vera Munro → died Fort Worth 1949 (Greenwood Memorial)
- Harry Munro → died Fort Worth 1962 (University Christian Church memorial)
- Frances Virginia Munro → died Fort Worth 2011, buried Greenwood Memorial Park
- Robert Barclay Hanson II → died Fort Worth 1958, buried Greenwood Memorial Park (same cemetery as Virginia)

Both Bob and Virginia are buried in the **same Fort Worth cemetery**. Bob died at 41 — ten years after the family relocated to Fort Worth. Virginia lived as a widow from 1958 to 1967, then remarried Vern Whittaker.

### The Greenbrier County Gap

The **Hanson Burial Ground at Piercys Mill, Greenbrier County WV exists and is indexed** — 3 interments confirmed. The direct connection between this cemetery and Robert Barclay Hanson II's documented line is **not yet established**. His grandfather is buried in Maryland. The WV cemetery predates modern genealogy indexing and may represent an older branch of the family. The 3 names behind the login wall are the clearest path to resolving this.
