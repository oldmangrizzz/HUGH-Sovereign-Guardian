## SECTION 6: THE HUMAN LOOP — EXACTLY WHERE YOU MUST BE

You specified "human off-loop but not fully." Here are the four specific gates. These are the only four places you must show up.

**Gate 1 — Scope Authorization (before anything runs)**
You sign off on every target in writing before any scan begins. Format: "I authorize assessment of [target]. Scope limited to: [what]. Explicitly excluded: [what]. Client authorization on file as of [date]." One sentence per scope element. This is your legal protection and your ethics gate. Nothing runs without this signature.

**Gate 2 — Findings Review (before delivery)**
The AI drafts the full report. You read it. You are not checking technical accuracy — the scanning tools handle that. You are asking: "Does this make sense? Is there anything in here that could harm someone? Do I stand behind this?" You approve or send it back for revision.

**Gate 3 — Delivery Authorization (final step)**
You hit send on the client report. Not the AI. Not an automated system. You. This keeps your professional stake in the output and your name attached to the deliverable.

**Gate 4 — Anomaly Escalation (anything unusual)**
If the system encounters a potential critical finding, something outside expected scope, anything touching patient data or financial records, or any result that doesn't make sense — it stops and waits. It does not draft. It does not proceed. It surfaces the anomaly to you first.

**The AI operates autonomously between these gates.** Running scans, interpreting output, categorizing findings, drafting the full report, tracking engagement status, managing the queue. You show up four times per engagement. Everything in between is handled.

## SECTION 7: SAFETY GATES — NON-NEGOTIABLE

These are the minimum viable equivalents of the Red Hood Protocol. They apply to every version of Aegis Forge, stripped or full.

**1. Written Scope Before Anything Runs**  
No signed scope authorization = no engagement. No exceptions. Even for friends. Even for yourself. The legal protection and the ethics gate are the same document.

**2. Assessment Tools vs. Exploitation Tools — Hard Separation**  
Vulnerability scanners (nuclei, nmap, Nessus) = authorized for standard assessments.  
Exploitation frameworks (Metasploit, BeEF, SET, similar) = require separate explicit written authorization stating the client specifically wants active exploitation testing, not just assessment. This is a different contract, a different scope, a different price. Keep these gates physically separate.

**3. Immutable Audit Log**  
Every scan, every finding, every report version, every delivery timestamp gets logged and does not get deleted. Alfred's archive function from the full design — you are implementing a simplified version from day one. If a client disputes a finding or a legal question arises, the log is your defense.

**4. PII Discovery = Stop and Surface**  
If the scan discovers personally identifiable information in any form — patient records, financial data, credentials, social security numbers — the system logs the discovery and stops. The report notes the finding. The client handles remediation. You do not extract, store, copy, or transmit PII. Not even to the report.

**5. One Active Authorization Per Target**  
No running multiple engagements against overlapping scopes without explicit separate authorizations for each. Scope creep is how liability happens.

**Why these specifically:** These five gates do not require Jason, Dick, Babs, or Alfred to exist. They do not require physiological state monitoring. They are procedural controls that a human operator can enforce without any infrastructure beyond a paper trail.
