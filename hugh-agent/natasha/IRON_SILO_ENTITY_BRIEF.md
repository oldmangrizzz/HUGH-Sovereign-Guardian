## THE THREE STRUCTURAL OPTIONS

### Option A: Stay as DBA Sole Proprietor *(Current State — No Action Required)*

**What it is:** You already have this. Grizzly Medicine is a registered trade name operating under your SSN/EIN as a sole proprietor.

**Pros:**
- Zero cost, zero paperwork, zero wait time
- You have it *right now*
- Sufficient for NAIRR, Longview/Astera, ASI Alliance, HF, Filecoin, Ethereum
- Simple taxes — Schedule C

**Cons:**
- Personal liability isn't separated from business
- Some institutional grants (NIH, NSF direct) require LLC or nonprofit
- Mozilla + OpenAI People-First *may* still want a more formal entity (needs direct verification)
- Limited credibility signal to deeply institutional funders

**Verdict:** **Use this for the entire current grant queue.** Don't spend time on formation when you're already formed.

---

### Option B: Single-Member LLC *(Upgrade Path, ~$50–$300, 1–3 weeks)*

**What it is:** Register Grizzly Medicine LLC with Texas Secretary of State. Your EIN carries over or you get a new one — either way, IRS treats it as "disregarded entity" (same taxes as sole prop unless you elect S-corp).

**Pros:**
- Personal liability protection
- Stronger credibility signal to institutional funders
- Unlocks Mozilla Foundation (requires legal entity)
- Unlocks OpenAI People-First Fund (requires organization)
- Unlocks future contracts with hospitals, health systems
- One-time cost: ~$300 TX filing fee + minimal annual maintenance

**Cons:**
- Takes 1–4 weeks
- Annual Texas franchise tax report (but $0 tax if revenue <$1.18M)
- Slightly more complex taxes (still Schedule C as disregarded entity)

**Verdict:** **Do this in May or June when grant applications are submitted** — not urgent for the current writing sprint, but high ROI before the October deadline.

---

### Option C: 501(c)(3) Nonprofit *(Long-term only — DO NOT DO NOW)*

**What it is:** Tax-exempt nonprofit status. Required for NIH R01s, NSF grants, most federal institutional grants.

**Pros:**
- Maximum grant eligibility
- Donation tax deductibility (donor incentive)
- Highest credibility tier

**Cons:**
- 6–18 months to approve
- $600 IRS filing fee + legal costs ($1,500–$5,000 if using attorney)
- Ongoing compliance: board requirements, Form 990, bylaws
- **Incompatible with the October deadline** — this is a 2027 path

**Verdict:** **Not now. Build the revenue first. Convert later if warranted.**

---

### Option D: Fiscal Sponsorship *(Emergency Bypass — No Longer Needed)*

**What it is:** Umbrella organization (e.g., Open Collective, NUMFOCUS, Code for Science & Society) receives grant funds on your behalf, takes 5–15% cut.

**Previously considered because:** Lab lacked any formal entity.

**Now:** DBA + EIN makes this unnecessary for the current queue. Fiscal sponsorship is overhead you don't need to pay.

**Verdict:** **Off the table. You outgrew this before you needed it.**
