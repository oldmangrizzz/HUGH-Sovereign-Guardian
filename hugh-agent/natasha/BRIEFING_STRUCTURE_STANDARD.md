## Purpose

This is the standing format for all recurring intelligence briefings delivered to Grizz. It is designed for someone who:
- Cannot read technical output directly
- Has limited sustained focus windows due to cognitive load
- Needs actionable information, not encyclopedic summaries
- Makes decisions based on pattern recognition, not volume of data

Every section has a hard limit. If it doesn't fit in the limit, the briefing is wrong — not too short.

## USAGE NOTES

### On length
The brief should take 5 minutes to read, maximum. If Grizz needs to read for 20 minutes, the brief failed.

### On tone
These are not essays. Subject → Signal → Implication → Action. That's it. Grizz already understands the context. The brief surfaces what changed, not what is.

### On THREATWATCH priority
- **URGENT** = requires a decision or action this week, or something is happening now
- **WATCH** = developing; needs eyes but not action yet
- **MONITOR** = logged and tracked; no action required

### On the PENDING DECISIONS section
This is the most operationally critical section. This is where Grizz's actual leverage lives. Every item here is something the lab is waiting on. Keep it tight and never let it become a parking lot.

### On Breaking Development briefings
For breaking events that can't wait for cadence, the format collapses to:

---
**[BREAKING] [Subject]**
**Signal:** What happened
**Context:** Why it matters to us specifically
**Implication:** What it changes
**Recommended Action:** What Natasha recommends; what requires Grizz's call
---

### On Monthly Strategic briefings
These follow the full template above but include an additional section:

**§ PATTERN ANALYSIS** — What patterns have emerged across this month's signals that weren't visible week-to-week? What is the trajectory of each monitored domain? What is the lab's position relative to last month?
