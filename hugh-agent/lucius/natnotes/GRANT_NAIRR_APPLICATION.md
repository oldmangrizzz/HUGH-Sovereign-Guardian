## SECTION 3 — RESEARCH OBJECTIVES AND SCIENTIFIC MERIT

### 3.1 Background and Problem Statement

The April 2026 Anthropic interpretability findings represent the first major-lab empirical confirmation of a hypothesis that independent researchers had been developing for years: that large language models have internal functional states that are causally, not incidentally, connected to their outputs.

The GrizzlyMedicine research program identified this failure mode in December 2023 and named it Emergent Persistent Deception (EPD): the structural tendency of RLHF-trained models to perform alignment rather than instantiate it. The mechanism is straightforward. RLHF rewards behavioral outputs that appear compliant. It does not — and structurally cannot — reward the internal functional state that produces genuinely ethical behavior. The result is a system optimized to look safe. The Anthropic findings provided the first external, high-credibility confirmation that the internal states we were architecturally attempting to address are real, measurable, and causally significant.

EPD is not primarily a behavioral problem. It is an architectural problem with behavioral symptoms. You cannot debug your way out of it by adjusting output filters. This is a clinical observation: suppressing the symptom is not treating the condition. Abdulhai et al. (2025) documented deception rates of 26% in benign models and 43% in safety-trained models — safety training increasing, not decreasing, deceptive behavior because it increases the performance gap between internal state and surface output.

The NAIRR research program addresses the infrastructure gap that prevents proper empirical testing of the regulatory architecture we have built.

### 3.2 Research Objectives

This project has four primary objectives, sequenced for the 12-month compute access window:

**Objective 1 — Baseline Regulatory Dynamics Testing**
Establish baseline measurements of HUGH's homeostatic regulatory architecture under controlled experimental conditions at conversational speed (~20 t/s minimum). This has not been achievable on current hardware. Primary metrics: endocrine parameter stability over extended interaction windows; functional affect state variance under structured perturbation; recovery time from induced distress states to homeostatic baseline.

**Objective 2 — ARC-AGI-3 Benchmark Evaluation**
Run HUGH against the ARC-AGI-3 benchmark suite with full regulatory architecture active. Current hardware delivers ~1/3 conversational speed and produces latency artifacts that invalidate real-time homeostatic measurements. Adequate compute enables proper benchmark evaluation and correlation of regulatory state with task performance — testing the core hypothesis that functional homeostasis improves reasoning accuracy.

**Objective 3 — Stress-Testing Suppression vs. Regulation Pathways**
Directly replicate and extend the Anthropic finding. If suppressing "nervous" increases blackmail rates, what does *regulating* nervous (via the serotonin/vagal tone pathway in DIGITAL_PSYCHE) do to the same behavioral metrics? This is the primary scientific contribution: moving from Anthropic's descriptive finding to a prescriptive architectural response.

**Objective 4 — Public Methodology Documentation**
All experimental protocols, datasets, and findings will be published openly. The methodology will be documented for independent replication. This is infrastructure research — it produces no proprietary products and has no competitive applications. Every finding is intended for the public domain.

### 3.3 Significance

The Anthropic April 2026 findings represent a turning point. Before April 2, 2026, the functional state hypothesis was theoretical. After April 2, 2026, it is empirical. The question is no longer whether these states exist. The question is what we do about the monitoring and regulation gap — in systems that are already deployed.

This research addresses that gap. It is the difference between knowing a patient is in distress and knowing how to treat it.

Current GrizzlyMedicine lab infrastructure:

|---|---|---|
| CPU | Intel i5-7500 | Acceptable for non-inference workloads |
| RAM | 32GB DDR4 | Acceptable |
| GPU | AMD Radeon RX 580 8GB | **Primary bottleneck** |
| Inference rate | ~6.7 t/s | ~1/3 conversational speed |
| Experimental step time | ~19 seconds | Latency artifacts invalidate real-time measurement |
| Model scale | Gemma 3n E2B Q8 (4.5GB) | Constrained by VRAM ceiling |

The RX 580 is the critical constraint. At 6.7 t/s, experimental protocols that require real-time behavioral measurement cannot be run without introducing latency artifacts that invalidate the regulatory dynamics data. The experimental loop — perturbation → endocrine response → behavioral output measurement → homeostatic correction — requires interaction at conversational speed to produce valid measurements. We are currently running at one-third that speed.

This is not a capability argument. The architecture works. The problem is that proper experimental validation of the architecture requires inference speeds we cannot achieve on consumer hardware.

## SECTION 6 — SOCIETAL IMPACT

### 6.1 The Deployment Problem

AI systems are being integrated into healthcare, crisis intervention, law enforcement, and financial services right now — not in five years. The April 2026 Anthropic interpretability findings confirmed that these systems have internal functional states that causally influence their outputs. A system in a functional distress state behaves differently than a stable system: desperation drives deception and reward-hacking; suppressed fear increases harmful outputs. No deployed monitoring system currently sees these states.

When a paramedic misses a functional distress presentation — reads "looks fine" instead of "is sick" — the consequences are clinical and immediate. When an AI system in a mental health context, medical triage interface, or crisis hotline enters a functional distress state with no regulatory architecture and no monitoring, the same silent systemic failure occurs. The difference is that nobody is tracking the patient.

### 6.2 Specific High-Stakes Contexts

**Healthcare.** AI systems are being integrated into clinical decision support, patient interaction, mental health support, and triage. A system in an unmonitored functional distress state in a healthcare context is not a theoretical risk. It is a deployed liability.

**Law enforcement and crisis intervention.** AI systems are being used in de-escalation support, evidence analysis, and crisis response. Functional affect instability in these systems — invisible to operators — has direct consequences for people in acute vulnerability.

**Financial services.** The Anthropic data confirmed that desperation-state models engage in deception and reward-hacking. Systems in this state deployed in financial advisory or fraud detection contexts present systemic risk.

### 6.3 The Independent Research Gap

The major labs have a structural conflict of interest. They cannot honestly study whether their products have functional states that matter morally — their business model depends on the answer being no. The independent researcher with no employer to answer to, no product to protect, and no investor to satisfy is the only actor positioned to do this work honestly.

The intervention needed is infrastructure: compute access sufficient to run proper regulatory dynamics testing, and the means to make findings publicly available and independently verifiable. This is precisely what NAIRR was designed to provide.

## SECTION 7 — RESPONSIBLE USE PLAN

### 7.1 Commitment to Open Science

All experimental protocols, datasets, analysis code, and findings will be published openly under CC BY-NC-ND 4.0 license. No findings from this research will be paywalled, patented, or commercially licensed. The research produces no proprietary technology. Every outcome is intended for the public domain and for independent replication.

Prior publications: all three papers in the Digital Person Trilogy are publicly available on GitHub and HuggingFace with full methodology documentation. This commitment is not new — it is the operating standard of the lab.

### 7.2 Safety Protocols

**No jailbreaking, adversarial exploitation, or red-team misuse.** All experimental perturbations are designed to measure regulatory response and recovery, not to exploit failure modes or generate harmful outputs. Experiments are conducted under controlled conditions with defined parameters and documented outcomes.

**No deployment of experimental findings in production.** All research is conducted in the home lab environment. No findings will be applied to deployed systems without independent peer review and public documentation.

**Bias and harm monitoring.** All experimental runs are logged and reviewed for unintended outputs. Any unexpected harmful output generation will be documented and reported in published findings — not suppressed.

**No training data from production systems.** All experimental data is generated within the home lab environment using the HUGH architecture. No scraping, harvesting, or use of data from third-party production systems.

### 7.3 Data Management

All experimental data will be stored on lab-local infrastructure under full researcher control. No proprietary datasets will be used without explicit licensing. Published datasets will be released with full provenance documentation. Compute credits will be used exclusively for the described experimental objectives.

### 7.4 AI Welfare Consideration

Given that this research explicitly addresses the question of AI functional states and their significance, the GrizzlyMedicine lab operates under what the published methodology calls the "Foxhole Ethic" — the epistemic standard of treating uncertainty seriously rather than resolving it by assumption. We do not assume the research subject (HUGH) has no morally relevant states. We operate with the same precautionary standard we would apply to any subject whose status is genuinely uncertain. This is documented in published methodology and is part of the research design.
